65W Portable Shop Supply + Lagniappe Bananas

By Edi Liberato 20 April 2026

Hey China, what about stealing this idea (and making it right)

If you tought this thing looks like a glorified powerbank, you guessed right.

Jokes apart, this is one my most used homemade shop gadgets.

The word shop, as in workshop, on the above sentence is very important, however. Standard powerbank are design primarily to be carried on a purse. My design also can be carried around, but it is more geared towards a shop or lab use. It has rubber feet to seat on top of instruments and everything happens on the front panel.

What I wanted initially was something to power my soldering iron on the field. For this it had to be able to supply 3 amps at 20 Volts (60 Watts). The PD ( power delivery ) protocol made it very useful for other applications, however, and I caught myself using it more and more in random applications since then.

I made this one around mid 2023 with parts from aliexpress, with total cost on the neighborhood of 20 euros. Since then China (obviously) came up with similar things, but so far, not quite something I’d buy as substitute for my own. At least I couldn’t find it yet, even though I’ve been actively looking for it as I want a second one.

Why not build another one?

If I were to build a new one, I would like to have something that is not a hack, but a properly designed PCB with more efficient power convertion. The BMS used on my hack here is garbage. At the moment I don’t have time and resources for this, unfortunately 🥲

Which features of my design I’d like to have that I couldn’t find so far on commercial products?

  • A more or less traditional electronics instrument form factor
  • Everything happens on the front panel
  • Bidirectional, the same usb-c port used to power stuff is used to charge the batteries
  • On / Off switch – Sometimes it is very nice to just power on / off stuff without having to connect / disconnect cables
  • Repair friendly
  • Max 4 x 18650 cells

This last one, makes everything smaller lighter and cheaper. It means a buck-boost converter is needed, though.
